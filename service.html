Service

Service matters to me because strong communities are built when people share knowledge, support one another, and take initiative. Below are a few ways I contribute now and how I plan to keep contributing through tutoring, mentoring, and engineering communities.

How I Give Back

Tutoring / Mentoring
I help students build strong fundamentals by breaking problems into smaller steps and explaining the “why” behind each concept. My goal is to improve confidence and problem-solving habits, not just finish an assignment.

External link: Blinn College Tutoring Center
Student Organizations
I’m interested in being active in engineering communities (example: IEEE) to learn from others, attend technical talks, and contribute to team projects. Professional communities help people grow and give back.

External link: ieee.org
Community Volunteering
I plan to keep supporting education and local communities through volunteering, especially events that help younger students explore STEM and understand what engineering looks like in real life.

External link: tamu.edu

Professional Service Goals

Documentation & Team Support
In engineering teams, clear documentation is a form of service. I want to support teams by writing clean notes, keeping files organized, and making it easier for others to understand decisions and reproduce results.

External link: git-scm.com
Helping Others Debug
I enjoy helping peers debug issues and understand tools. Small guidance—like explaining errors or showing a better workflow—can save someone hours and improve the whole group’s progress.

External link: stackoverflow.com
Sharing Project Write-ups
Long-term, I want to publish simple project write-ups that explain what I built, what failed, and what I learned. That way, future students can learn faster and avoid common mistakes.

External link: developer.mozilla.org
